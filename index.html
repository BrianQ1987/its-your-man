Oh, it's your man!

A website about revered character actor Richard Kind

Despite having acted in over 59 movies, Richard Kind is not a name that many people would recognise. He does, however have one of those faces that when you see it you go: "Oh, it's your man!"

Whether it's playing hypochondriac patient Harvey Corman on four episodes of Scrubs or providing the voice of Bing Bong in Pixar's Inside Out each performance given by Richard Kind is memorable but not memorable enough to stop you getting out your phone, going to IMDb just to remind yourself "What do I know him from?"

Below, is some information on his most memorable roles. Click on each of the titles to expand for more information.

Inside Out (2015)

Inside Out (2015)

Richard Kind plays the role of Bing Bong, Riley's imaginary friend. Social, cheerful and lively - it's the role Richard Kind was born to play!

We meet Riley at a time in her life when she is speaking to Bing Bong less and less. This doesn't stop Bing Bong from helping Riley's emotions on their quest to help restore Riley's happiness.

Ultimately Bing Bong sacrifices his life for Riley and fades away in her memory dump. Forgotten forever. Much like the name "Richard Kind".

A Serious Man (2009)

A Serioius Man (2009)

It's not just major studios who are ambling to include Richard Kind in their projects, even acclaimed indie directors the Coen Brothers need a little Kindness in their lives.

Here, Richard Kind plays Arthur Gopnik, the gambling addicted, sleep apnea suffering brother to lead character Larry Gopnik.

When he isn't annoying Larry's family, Arthur spend a lot of his time scribbling in his notebook in order to derive what he calls the Mentaculus - a probability map of the unvierse. It makes one wonder: what are the odds you will remember the name Richard Kind after visiting this website?

Only Murders in The Building (2021-Present)

Only Murders in the Building (2021-Present)

In its fourth season the big brains at Disney needed a fresh mystery to entice its fans with.

And what better mystery than who was the actor playing the mysterious, eye-patch sporting, West Tower resident, Vince Fish?

The answer is, of course, veteran characer actor Richard Kind!
